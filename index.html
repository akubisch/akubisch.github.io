I am leaving academia.

In June 2017 I will bow out of research and start a new life in a private company, which I am very much looking forward to. This is why I shortly want to lay out my reasons for giving up my previous life and start anew.

Certainly, you all know that the conditions of employment at universities are continuously getting worse. I finished my Ph.D. when I was 26 years old. I was excited and motivated, looking forward to pursuing a career in science. After I even won a prize for my work and got a job in a renowned institute for evolutionary biology in one of the world's most beautiful cities, I knew that this was my way to go.

Meanwhile, things have changed. After five years as a postdoc with regular moving and starting over I simply cannot continue. Except for getting a professorship (for which I am too young), there is no alternative way of getting a permanent contract in Germany. I love my job. But even more so do I want to build up a happy and stable life with my wife. This is why I say good-bye to this system.

After almost a decade of research, I do, however, look back at this time full of gratitude. I met great people, visited amazing places and worked on fascinating projects. Of course, I hope that my publications may inspire future work.

Finally, there is only one thing left to say: Thank you so much to everyone I met during this time. Thank you for our common experiences and fruitful joint work. I hope, we'll meet again!

Wishing you all the very best,
Alex Kubisch
